Client Services (BAZ)

The Bayt Al-Zahra Urgent Needs Program, Zaman’s flagship initiative, gives food, clothing, shelter, and other forms of emergency relief to marginalized women and children, senior populations, and the terminally ill.

An official partner of Gleaners Community Food Bank, Zaman’s Client Choice Food Pantry allows families to choose culturally appropriate and nutritionally balanced food each month. The Mobile Hot Food Pantry works with partner restaurants and dedicated volunteers to deliver hot, nutritious meals year-round to clients without transportation.

General description of our social work department, what the goals are for the clients.

Our program offers case management and support services for single female heads of household and refugee families that have recently arrived. Our hope is to help our clients overcome obstacles they may be facing by providing basic needs services, encouragement, and access to other resources and referrals. Together with a social worker, clients will discuss their strengths and set goals to work on. Clients meet with a social worker every three months to discuss their progress towards their goals. Taking action and attempting to make progress is required to continue in good standing in the program.

Crisis Assistance

Client Choice Food Pantry and Client Choice Closet

An official partner of Gleaners Community Food Bank, Zaman’s Client Choice Food Pantry allows families to choose culturally appropriate and nutritionally balanced food each month. For homebound clients, we do deliveries of our basic services, especially food, and the results of the coat, backpack, turkey, and holiday drives.

Integration/refugee assistance program

Refugee clients are eligible for a maximum of six months of services.

Mobile Food Pantry

The Mobile Hot Food Pantry works with partner restaurants and dedicated volunteers to deliver hot, nutritious meals year-round to clients without transportation. For those families who would not otherwise have access to a square meal, we do weekly hot food deliveries. Clients most in need are put on a schedule, and volunteers pick up the food that students made in the kitchen that day and deliver it to that evening’s schedule.

Plots for Tots Infant Burial Program

This program assists families that cannot afford a proper burial for their fetus, infant or toddler.

Basic Eligibility

  • Must be female single headed households living within our financial scale. Head of house must be looking for a life change and willing to participate in the case management portion of our program. This will require them to engage with a case worker at least every 3 months to set goals, discuss strengths and challenges, and create a plan to reach their goals. Progress must be made between sessions. Clients are also encouraged to join the BOOST program as part of their goals and to attain a new strength and skill.

Required documents

  • ID or birth certificates for all individuals in the house
  • All forms of monthly income including any financial assistance (food stamps, ssi, child support, etc)
  • Rent agreements
  • All monthly expenses
  • Proof of any disability in the home is also required
